<p><p>Oncology ablation encompasses various techniques for destroying cancer cells, with Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA) and Microwave Ablation (MWA) being prominent methods. RFA utilizes heat generated by high-frequency radio waves to target and eliminate tumors, commonly employed for liver and kidney cancers. MWA, on the other hand, uses electromagnetic microwaves to produce intense heat, offering faster and more effective results, particularly in larger tumors. Both methods are minimally invasive and enhance treatment options for patients with localized malignancies.</p></p>
