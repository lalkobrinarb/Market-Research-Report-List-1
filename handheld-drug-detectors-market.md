<p><p>Handheld drug detectors encompass various technologies, including IR spectrometers, which identify substances by analyzing their infrared absorption patterns. Mass Spectrometry (MS) offers precise molecular analysis by measuring mass-to-charge ratios of ions. Ion Mobility Spectrometry (IMS) separates ions based on mobility in a gas, providing rapid identification. Gas Chromatography separates components in a mixture for detailed analysis, while Raman Spectrometry uses laser light to detect molecular vibrations, aiding in the characterization of substances. Each method enhances field-deployment capabilities for drug detection.</p></p>
