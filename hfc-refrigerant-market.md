<p><p>The HFC refrigerant market comprises different types based on their chemical composition and application. R12, an older CFC refrigerant, was phased out due to environmental concerns. R22, an HCFC, is still used but faces bans in many regions due to its ozone-depleting potential. R23, a trifluoromethane, is primarily used in low-temperature applications and has a substantial global warming potential. Other HFCs include R404A and R410A, commonly used in cooling systems but also targeted for reduction due to climate impact.</p></p>
