<p><p>The osmotic laxative market encompasses various agents used to alleviate constipation by drawing water into the intestines. Lactulose is a synthetic sugar that enhances bowel movements by promoting water retention. Sorbitol, a sugar alcohol, functions similarly, creating a softer stool. Magnesium citrate is a saline laxative that stimulates bowel activity. Milk of magnesia, containing magnesium hydroxide, acts by attracting water to the intestines. Polyethylene glycol is a non-absorbable compound, effectively increasing stool moisture and improving passage.</p></p>
