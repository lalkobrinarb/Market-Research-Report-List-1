<p><p>The Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) wafer market is primarily segmented into LEC (Liquid Encapsulated Czochralski) grown GaAs and VGF (Vertical Gradient Freeze) grown GaAs. LEC grown GaAs wafers are produced using a melt growth process, offering high purity and large diameter wafers suitable for applications in high-frequency and optoelectronic devices. VGF grown GaAs wafers, on the other hand, utilize a solidification method that enhances thermal properties and uniformity, making them ideal for advanced semiconductor applications like solar cells and lasers.</p></p>
