<p><p>The biodegradable chelates market consists of various types, including EDTA, EDDHA, DTPA, IDHA, and others. EDTA (ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid) is widely used for metal ion chelation in environmental and agricultural applications. EDDHA (ethylene diamine-N,N'-diacetic acid) is preferred for iron chelation in alkaline soils. DTPA (diethylenetriaminepentaacetic acid) effectively chelates heavy metals, while IDHA (isopropylidene diacetic acid) is naturally derived and eco-friendly. Other chelates may include newer formulations and natural variations, catering to specific industrial and agricultural needs for sustainable solutions.</p></p>
